Prologue

I’ve been living with the Misteros, in upstate New York, for about two months. For the most part, I like it. They welcomed me into their whole family! It’s crazy how big American families are. They have given me a long, white, robe that goes down to my feet, special arm bands, and a wrap for my ears. I miss my mama, but I’m sure my friends will find her soon.

one of the preacher’s apprentices and I, Silkvre talk sometimes. She speaks to me in English all the time, and I have to tell her over and over I don’t speak it yet! I sure hope I can learn English one day. I have to stick to Kezian for now. Silkvre watches horror movies with me. She likes them a whole lot. I can’t even see them because they scare me... Silkvre says that it doesn’t scare her at all! That’s crazy.

Silkvre’s mama is nice. She smokes cigarettes in the house though. That makes it hard for me to find my way around sometimes, since I’m blind, and smell and hearing are my main navigation tools, the smoking puts me at a bit of a disadvantage. Silkvre’s papa makes me a little nervous, but I understand it must be hard running a house. He calls me “retard boy”. That’s what Silkvre told me. I didn’t know what a retard was. Silkvre told me it was someone who was really dumb. That kind of hurt my feelings. It is probably because I can’t speak English. I guess that might be my fault, I mean I am living in their community, and I can’t even speak one of their main languages!

I’ve been going to Silkvre’s brother, Gold, to teach me. He teaches me basic words, and reads me Kezian sacred texts, translated into English. He prays a whole lot- Americans take their religion  very seriously. Every day they bring me to a building where Silkvre and her parents speak to the whole family about Keznivizdan. I was Kezian back in France, but it’s crazy here! Gold tells me the people who practice casually like my mama will not go where we’re going when we die. He says only the most devoted Kezians go to where good people go. His family saves people, so they can go there. That sounds very nice. I’m glad for them. Maybe when we find my mama they can save her too.

Gold is a nice boy. He always seems to be wearing his religious attire. He says he always needs to pray whenever he can, so Keznivizdan will save him. He wears a necklace, the dice hanging around his neck. My mom gave me a similar one back in France.

 Another thing about Americans is that they don’t seem to be afraid of anything. Silkvre had me go into her basement and hide in a cabinet, because she needed to speak to her parents alone, and it was family stuff that they didn’t want me to hear since I was just a family friend. Silkvre came back later and she had a black eye! She had said she went out fighting the creatures of the forest without me after her parents talked to her about morning plans, and a bear got her right in the eye!! She said she killed it. I wish I was brave like her.

 For a girl, she really acts like a boy. I asked Gold why Sil acts like that when he is so quiet, and he said that is trying to be special, and that if she doesn’t stop she won’t go to where the good people go. I went to tell her about her brother’s advice, and she cursed at me. It scared me, because kids aren’t allowed to curse. She and her brother must have different ways of doing things, but I hear that’s how siblings are.
